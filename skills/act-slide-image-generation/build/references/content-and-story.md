# Content And Story

## Causal composition

Choose the exhibit from the relationship the audience must understand. Allow the evidence to determine whether the composition is linear, layered, comparative, spatial, sequential, immersive, or hybrid. Use scale, alignment, proximity, contrast, and whitespace to communicate hierarchy. Integrate the implication where it naturally completes the reading experience.

Review the composition abstractly across topics. The exhibit passes when a viewer can identify where to start, what changes or contrasts, and what implication follows without relying on decorative symmetry. Repair by clarifying direction, strengthening the dominant object, merging a detached conclusion into the exhibit, reducing radial label placement and long vertical text, and redistributing the occupied regions to match the intended center of gravity.

Body improvements retain the approved deck master. Compare every revised slide with the pilot for H1/subtitle geometry, left anchor, canvas tone, palette roles, outer shell, and footer mode; preserve the improved logic while restoring any drift in the shared master.

## Single-message canvas review

Review header, upper body, lower body, and footer as one continuous canvas. Each occupied region supports the governing thought and participates in a comprehensible reading experience. Multiple framed exhibits, coordinated regions, diptychs, and small multiples are available when their relationship is clear. Treat duplicated headers, logically disconnected exhibits, competing independent narratives, or content copied from a reference page as structural repair issues.

Use this file for brief-to-deck reasoning.

Apply `argument-closure-and-deck-logic.md` before selecting visual grammar. Treat the deck as a dependency-complete decision argument and each slide as a closed reasoning unit whose claim, evidence, warrant, implication, and action or transition are explicit.

## Deck Contract

Resolve purpose, audience, required decision, intended use, language, source strictness, and palette. Use embedded ACT defaults when the brief supports them; clarify decisions that materially change the result.

## Story Logic

1. State one governing thought.
2. Map setup, tension, proof turn, and landing.
3. Write action titles whose read-through forms one argument.
4. Give every slide a clear visual hierarchy whose number and arrangement of focal regions follow the evidence.
5. Match every title to a named exhibit element that proves it.
6. Check that supporting content is sufficient, non-redundant, and logically connected; choose the reasoning form that fits the argument.
7. Register the strongest plausible alternative and the boundary of each decision-critical claim.
8. Confirm that every slide conclusion is used downstream and that the final page carries owners, timing, and success measures.

## Ordinal Semantics

Use ordinal markers when order carries meaning and parallel grouping when elements are peers. Choose numbering, alignment, labels, shape, or spatial position according to the relationship the reader needs to perceive.

## Evidence And State

Classify load-bearing content as fact, estimate, assumption, anecdote, or open. Render actuals with solid emphasis and future/plan/target states with pale or dashed treatment plus labels. Use `Source: ` only for a published, independently traceable external reference. Use `Assumption: ` for a decision-relevant management premise and `Note: ` for a concise methodology or scope qualification. Combine the active entries in the canonical provenance footer; retain detailed internal material in the source ledger and speaker notes.

## Density

Plan density through the measurable body-width, body-height, readability, and centroid contract. Increase decision value through evidence and context that serve the argument, while preserving readable type and intentional whitespace.

## Readability and meaningful sequence

Use WCAG-backed readability targets for visible slide text: >=4.5:1 contrast for normal text and >=3:1 for large text, Japanese paragraph lines <=40 CJK glyphs, ragged-right alignment, and 1.4-1.5 line height for multi-line explanatory copy. Give each slide a unique descriptive action title. Declare one reading path before layout and verify that arrows, numbering, alignment, and grouping preserve the same sequence. Pair color with labels, shape, position, or pattern.

For quantitative comparisons, prefer common-baseline position, bars, or aligned dots. Keep area, angle, pictogram size, and decorative icons as secondary encodings. Place the decision-carrying number directly by its mark and label series directly when space permits.

## Visual Grammar

Choose the structure that makes the claim easiest to see: comparison, table, flow, roadmap, loop, matrix, KPI bridge, evidence strip, architecture stack, or restrained illustration. Use icons as sparse wayfinding. Use `single_focus_fill` as a selective decision signal and `primary_accent` as the stable structural accent.

After choosing the visual grammar, express it through the mandatory layout tree in `grid-flex-layout.md`. Use Grid for relationships that require both row and column alignment. Use Flex for ordered distribution along one axis inside a declared region. Preserve content-led variety through different spans, nesting, layering, axes, and growth priorities while keeping every placement and free-space decision measurable.
