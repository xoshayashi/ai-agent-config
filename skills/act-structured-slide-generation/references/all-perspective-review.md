# All-Perspective Review

Use this file after the first render and before the independent rubric loop. A slide can look
clean from one angle and still fail from another. Review every substantive slide through all
20 lenses and record concrete evidence in `review-log.md`.

For each lens, answer the questions with observed facts from the render and `deck.json`.

## 1. Strategic Perspective

- What strategic belief, priority, or decision does this slide move?
- Does it clarify positioning, timing, advantage, risk, trade-off, or resource allocation?
- Is it more than a neutral fact introduction?

## 2. Audience Perspective

- Who is reading: investor, executive, customer, employee, partner, analyst, or public?
- Does the slide answer what this reader doubts, needs, or must decide?
- Can the reader understand the intended point in five seconds?

## 3. Narrative Perspective

- What role does the slide play in the deck's argument?
- Does it move the story forward rather than add loose information?
- Does it bridge from the previous page and set up the next one?

## 4. Business Perspective

- What business mechanism is explained: revenue, cost, gross margin, retention, growth,
  distribution, defensibility, or execution capacity?
- Are initiatives tied to the metrics they move?

## 5. Investor Perspective

- What can an investor underwrite from this page?
- Are assumptions and uncertainties visible?
- Is evidence strength proportional to claim strength?

## 6. Customer Perspective

- Is the customer reality expressed as behavior, workflow, constraint, pain, or desired
  outcome rather than generic adjectives?
- Is urgency or ROI clear?

## 7. Product Perspective

- Does the reader understand what exists, what is planned, and what remains unverified?
- Are screenshots, workflows, or architecture used as evidence rather than feature lists?

## 8. Market Perspective

- Are segment, timing, demand, and reachable opportunity connected to assumptions?
- Does the slide avoid confusing total market size with obtainable opportunity?

## 9. Competitive Perspective

- Are comparison axes fair and specific?
- Does the slide explain why the company wins, not merely why alternatives are weak?

## 10. Evidence Perspective

- Is every load-bearing claim backed by a fact, estimate, assumption, anecdote, or open marker?
- Are sources, dates, denominators, and definitions clear?

## 11. Data Perspective

- Is the chart/table type appropriate to the claim?
- Are values, units, labels, and time periods internally consistent?
- Does a chart require comparison, or should it become a hero number/gauge/table?

## 12. Design Perspective

- Is the design impact created by structure, scale, alignment, and hierarchy?
- Does freshness improve comprehension rather than decorate the page?

## 13. Copy Perspective

- Is the title a conclusion, not a topic?
- Are labels concrete, short, and free of generic adjectives?
- Does the slide avoid two competing claims?

## 14. Typography Perspective

- Can body evidence be read from the back row?
- Are title, heading, body, metric, metric subline, and source visually distinct?
- Is YoY/delta text separated from the value with enough line gap?

## 15. Layout Perspective

- Does the grid/flex contract show through in the render?
- Are role bands, object gaps, and alignment spines consistent?
- Is the body field neither sparse nor cramped?

## 16. Visual Asset Perspective

- Are images, logos, and screenshots real evidence?
- Are crops legible and relevant?
- Is any asset decorative or stock-like without proving the claim?

## 17. Brand Perspective

- Does the slide follow the Act palette, type hierarchy, and understated source discipline?
- Is color meaning stable across the deck?

## 18. Accessibility Perspective

- Can the main claim be read in thumbnail?
- Does the slide avoid relying on color alone?
- Are labels and contrast sufficient for a projector setting?

## 19. Production Perspective

- Does the editable PPTX preserve text, shapes, charts, and sources?
- Did validate, build, verify, render, and lint pass?
- Are there any renderer artifacts or font problems?

## 20. Review Perspective

- Are defects prioritized P0/P1/P2?
- Is each repair tied to a visible issue?
- Does the repair stay local, or does the deck need an outline-level change?
