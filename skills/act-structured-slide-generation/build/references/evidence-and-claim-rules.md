# Evidence And Claim Rules

Use this file before writing titles and before visual review. It governs how strongly the deck
may claim something.

## Claim-Evidence Binding

- One slide equals one claim.
- Place the strongest evidence closest to the claim.
- Anchor qualitative claims to at least one verifiable number, artifact, named case, or
  explicit assumption.
- Give the strongest evidence the strongest visual weight.
- Do not inflate weak evidence with design intensity.
- If one evidence set is asked to prove two claims, split the slide.

## Evidence Status

Classify every load-bearing item:

- **fact**: disclosed or verified actual data.
- **estimate**: model or external estimate with method/date.
- **assumption**: internal plan, target, scenario, or sensitivity driver.
- **anecdote**: case, quote, or example.
- **open**: needed but unsupported.

Actuals, forecasts, plans, targets, and assumptions must not look identical.

## Source Discipline

- Every number needs period, unit, denominator, and definition.
- External data needs organization, report or dataset, date, and denominator.
- Internal estimates and management plans belong in `assumption`, not `source`.
- Proprietary KPIs and non-GAAP metrics need formula and scope.
- Series breaks, accounting changes, reclassifications, and one-off effects need notes.
- Missing and not-applicable values should be distinguished.

## Trust Expressions

- Bad news uses the same grammar as good news: cause, action, recovery path.
- Superlatives require evidence: source, universe, date, and method.
- Future-looking claims use ranges, assumptions, or caveats instead of promise-like language.
- Third-party validation is stronger than self-assertion.
- Risk disclosure can strengthen credibility when it is specific and tied to action.

## Evidence By Perspective

### Investor

State what the investor is underwriting: market, growth, traction, unit economics, moat, GTM,
team, risk, or use of funds. Make assumptions and sensitivity drivers visible.

### Business

Show the value-creation mechanism, not only the outcome. Tie initiatives to revenue, cost,
margin, retention, growth, distribution, or defensibility.

### Customer

Show behavior, workflow, constraint, pain, urgency, ROI, or adoption change. Avoid abstract
pain statements.

### Product

Separate existing capability, planned capability, and unverified concept. Use screenshots and
workflows as evidence, not as feature wallpaper.

### Market

Separate total market from reachable opportunity. Tie sizing to assumptions and explain why
the opportunity opens now.

### Competitive

Use fair, specific axes. Prove why the company wins and what makes the advantage durable.
