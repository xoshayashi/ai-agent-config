# Coverage Universe

The skill should cover seed to pre-IPO startup finance by composing generic
modules. This is a coverage universe, not a routing table.

## Company Maturity

- Formation and seed: runway, founder ownership, option pool, grants, R&D burn,
  milestone financing, convertible instruments, and first pricing logic.
- Early commercialization: customer acquisition, pilots, conversion, pricing,
  unit economics, headcount plan, burn multiple, and next-round readiness.
- Scaling: cohorts, retention, gross margin, sales capacity, deployment
  capacity, working capital, capex, leases, debt capacity, and scenario range.
- Late private: multi-segment planning, consolidation, governance controls,
  valuation support, debt/equity trade-off, secondary, taxes, and IPO-readiness
  metrics.

## Business Mechanics

Map mechanics from facts, not labels:

- Recurring software and usage revenue.
- Marketplace, GMV, take rate, and two-sided liquidity.
- Hardware, RaaS, leasing, manufacturing, inventory, warranty, and service.
- Fintech lending, credit losses, regulatory capital, and loan book funding.
- Biotech/deeptech milestone, grants, trials, IP, and partnership revenue.
- Consumer, commerce, logistics, services, enterprise, and project-based
  revenue.

## Finance Surfaces

Include surfaces only when they answer the decision:

- Revenue build, cost build, people plan, capex, working capital, P&L, BS, CF.
- KPI and unit economics by the real economic unit.
- Capital stack, dilution, convertible conversion, option pool, secondary,
  taxes, venture debt, leases, covenants, and cash runway.
- Market support, comparables, valuation, DCF, exit multiple, SOTP, M&A exit,
  and investor memo.

## Modeling Standard

Every important row should have a unit, source status, formula or input status,
and decision relevance. If a metric is not meaningful for the current economics,
omit it or label why it is not decision-useful.

For key assumptions, require a second layer of logic when source support is
weak. The model should show the selected value, the explanatory variables that
would justify it, the implied value from those variables, and a ratio or
variance that tells the reviewer whether the selected value is adequately
supported. This applies especially to demand, pricing, retention, gross margin,
headcount, capex, runway, and financing capacity.
